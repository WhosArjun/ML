If we have a bunch of students and each of them have an exam
and each of these students invested a certain amount of time into studying
for the exam.

Study Time : 0h - inf(h)
Exam Score : Result of the test (0,100)

We want to plot this onto a 2d coordinat system
Y axis exam score, and the x axis is the study time

People who don't study a lot get the worst scores, people who study a little more
get better scores, and people who study more get really good scores.

What a Linear Regression tries to do is it tries to find a line that best fits all of these points.
Some care about outliers and others don't care about outliers.
Minimizing the error is what matters. The error is the distance from the prediction to the actual line.

We want to find a function that minimizes the error.

y = mx+b -> Linear Regression Equation

E = (1/n) * (the sum of i=0 up until n)(yi-(mx+b))^2
Mean Squared Error Function 

For each point until the n amount of points to the last point, is we get the y value of the actual point
and we substract the position of the function y value. Then we get the difference of them and square that difference.
    We then take all of these differences, square difference, divide by n.

We want to find the line that gives us the lowest possible E, we can only manipulate m and b.
We can take the partial derivitive in respect to m and respect to b.
    partial derivitive : Measures the rate of change of a multivariable function (like a 3d surface) in one specific direction.
    Imaging you are standing on a hilly landscape described by the function z = f(x,y). If you want to know how steep the hill is strictly
    walking forward (in the x direction), you use the partial derivative with respect to x, denoted as af/ax. To calculate it, you freeze the y coordinate,
    effectively turning the 3D surface into a simple 2d curve, and find its standard slope.

    Given the function f(x,y) = x^2 + y^3
    To find the partial derivative with respect to x(af/ax), treat y as a constant.
    The derivative of x^2 is 2x, and the derivative of the constant y^3 = 0. Therefore af/ax = 2x.

E = (1/n) * (the sum of i=0 up until n)(yi-(mx+b))^2
2E/2m = 1/n * i=0 to n (2*(yi-(m*xi+b))) 
    = -2/n * i=0 to n * xi(yi-(m*xi+b)) - in respect to m 

2E/2b = -2/n * sum from i to n (yi-(m*xi+b))
